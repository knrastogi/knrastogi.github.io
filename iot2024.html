Internet of Things (IoT)

Course Objective Syllabus Lecture Plan Reference Books Other Reference Materials Lab Exercises

Course Objective

This course aims to provide a thorough understanding of the Internet of Things (IoT), including its architecture, protocols, applications, and implementation. Students will learn to design IoT solutions and understand the integration of various IoT devices and platforms.

Syllabus

  • Introduction to IoT
  • IoT Architecture and Protocols
  • IoT Sensors and Actuators
  • IoT Data Management and Analytics
  • Edge, Fog and Cloud Computing
  • IoT Security and Privacy
  • IoT Applications and Case Studies
  • IoT Development Platforms
  • Future Trends in IoT

Lecture Plan

  • Week 1: Introduction to IoT and Its Applications
  • Week 2: IoT Reference Architecture
  • Week 3: Sensors and Actuators
  • Week 4: IoT Network Protocols
  • Week 5: IoT Development Platforms
  • Week 6: IoT Data Management and Analytics
  • Week 7: Edge, Fog and Cloud Computing
  • Week 8: IoT Issues and Challenges - Security and Privacy, Trust, Interoperability, Energy
  • Week 9: Software Defined Networks for IoT
  • Week 10: IoT Applications in Smart Homes and Cities, Healthcare and Industry
  • Week 12: Case Studies of IoT Implementations
  • Week 13: Future Trends and Emerging Technologies in IoT

  • “Architecting the Internet of Things” by Dieter Uckelmann, Mark Harrison, and Florian Michahelles
  • “Building the Internet of Things” by Maciej Kranz

Other Reference Materials
